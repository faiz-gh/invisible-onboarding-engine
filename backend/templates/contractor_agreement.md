# INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR AGREEMENT

**Date:** {date}

This Independent Contractor Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into between Deriv Limited (the "Client") and {name} (the "Contractor"). The parties agree as follows:

## 1. ENGAGEMENT
1.1 The Client engages the Contractor to provide professional services as described in the Scope of Services below. The Contractor accepts the engagement on the terms of this Agreement.

## 2. SCOPE OF SERVICES
2.1 Services: Contractor agrees to provide the services and deliverables described as: **{role}** and any ancillary tasks reasonably required to fulfil that role.
2.2 Change Control: Any material change to the scope, timetable, or deliverables will be agreed in writing and may be subject to a change in fees.

## 3. TERM
3.1 This Agreement commences on the date above and continues until the completion of the services or termination in accordance with section 9.

## 4. FEES AND EXPENSES
4.1 Fees: Client shall pay Contractor a fee of **{currency} {salary:,.2f}** per month, payable within 30 days of receipt of a correct invoice, unless otherwise agreed in writing.
4.2 Expenses: Contractor shall be reimbursed for reasonable pre-approved expenses incurred in performing the services upon submission of receipts.
4.3 Invoice Details: Invoices must reference the Client purchase order (if any), include reasonable detail of hours/deliverables, and be sent to the Client's accounts payable address.

## 5. TAXES AND BENEFITS
5.1 The Contractor is engaged as an independent contractor. The Contractor is solely responsible for all taxes, national insurance/social security contributions, and any benefits or statutory entitlements.

## 6. WARRANTIES AND STANDARDS
6.1 The Contractor represents and warrants that they have the necessary skills, licences and authority to perform the services to a professional standard and in compliance with applicable law.

## 7. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY
7.1 Ownership: All work product, inventions, designs, code, documentation and other materials created by the Contractor in connection with the services ("Work Product") will be the exclusive property of the Client upon full payment.
7.2 Moral Rights: To the extent permitted by law, the Contractor hereby waives and assigns any moral rights in the Work Product.

## 8. CONFIDENTIALITY AND DATA PROTECTION
8.1 Confidential Information: The Contractor shall keep confidential all non-public information disclosed by the Client and shall not use or disclose it except to perform the services.
8.2 Data Protection: Where Contractor processes personal data on behalf of the Client, the Contractor shall comply with applicable data protection laws and any written instructions provided by the Client.

## 9. TERMINATION
9.1 Either party may terminate this Agreement by giving 14 days' written notice to the other party.
9.2 Immediate Termination: The Client may terminate immediately for material breach, gross negligence, fraud, or if the Contractor becomes insolvent.
9.3 Effect of Termination: Upon termination, the Contractor will deliver all Work Product and invoices for fees and pre-approved expenses incurred to the date of termination.

## 10. INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR STATUS
10.1 The parties agree the Contractor is an independent contractor and nothing in this Agreement shall create an employer-employee relationship, partnership or joint venture.

## 11. LIABILITY AND INSURANCE
11.1 Liability: The Contractor's liability for any claim arising under or in connection with this Agreement shall be limited to the total fees paid by the Client in the 12 months preceding the claim, except for liability arising from wilful misconduct or gross negligence.
11.2 Insurance: The Contractor shall maintain adequate professional indemnity and public liability insurance where relevant and provide proof on request.

## 12. NON-SOLICITATION
12.1 During the term and for 6 months thereafter, the Contractor shall not solicit employees or contractors of the Client for work.

## 13. ASSIGNMENT
13.1 The Contractor may not assign or subcontract their obligations without the Client's prior written consent.

## 14. GOVERNING LAW AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION
14.1 Governing Law: This Agreement is governed by the laws of England and Wales.
14.2 Dispute Resolution: The parties will attempt to resolve disputes by good faith negotiation and, if unresolved, submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the English courts.
